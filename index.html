Project Background

The modern landscape of Natural Language Processing (NLP) remains heavily skewed toward high-resource languages like English. Philippine languages, including major dialects such as Tagalog, Cebuano, and Ilocano, distinctly exemplify the challenge of processing low-resource languages with rich, agglutinative morphology.

Ugat is an intelligent system specifically designed to accurately detect and transform inflected or derived words across major Philippine dialects into their fundamental root or base forms. By providing a dedicated solution for morphological processing, Ugat aims to bridge the gap and enhance NLP performance for these underserved languages.

The NLP Problem We Address

Philippine languages are characterized by complex word variations systematically derived from a single root through prefixes, infixes, suffixes, and reduplication. For example, the Tagalog root word basa (read) can transform into:

  • nagbasa — read (past tense)
  • bumabasa — reading (present tense)
  • babasahin — will be read (future tense)

This morphological complexity generates vast vocabulary permutations, making it difficult for standard NLP systems to process them efficiently.
